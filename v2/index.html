How the grid was built

1926 Central Electricity Board created — the idea of a national grid
1935 132kV "Gridiron" energised — first nationwide network
1947 Nationalisation — British Electricity Authority
1957 Calder Hall — first commercial nuclear power
1965 400kV Supergrid begins — the backbone we still use today
1990 Privatisation — CEGB split into PowerGen, National Power, NGC
1998 NETA replaces the Pool — bilateral trading begins
2001 BETTA extends trading to Scotland — single GB market
2013 Electricity Market Reform — CfDs and Capacity Market introduced
2017 First coal-free day in Britain (21 April)
2019 First coal-free week — and the 2050 net zero target legislated
Sep 2024 Last coal plant closes — Ratcliffe-on-Soar, after 57 years
Oct 2024 NESO created — system operator nationalised, separated from National Grid
Jan 2025 Greenlink interconnector to Ireland goes live
Jul 2025 REMA confirms single national wholesale price (rejects zonal pricing)
